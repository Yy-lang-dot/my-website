Nikola Tesla

A man who made significant contributions to the modern alternating current electrical system

  • 1856: Born in Smiljan, Austrian Empire (now Croatia).
  • 1875-1878: Studied at the Polytechnic School in Graz, Austria.
  • 1879: Worked in Maribor.
  • 1880-1883: Worked for Continental Edison in Paris and later in Budapest.
  • 1884: Immigrated to the United States and joined Thomas Edison's company.
  • 1887: Received funding from George Westinghouse and established his own company.
  • 1888: Tesla patented the AC induction motor and other AC systems.
  • 1889: Secured funding from George Westinghouse.
  • 1891: Patented the Tesla coil, a key element in radio technology.
  • 1893: Demonstrated the Tesla coil at the Chicago World's Fair.
  • 1896: Invented the first ozone generator and founded the Tesla Ozone Company.
  • 1898: Demonstrated the first remote-controlled boat in New York.
  • 1899: Built a laboratory in Colorado Springs to conduct high-voltage experiments.
  • 1900-1901: Worked on the Wardenclyffe Tower, a radio communication tower.
  • 1917: The Wardenclyffe Tower was destroyed for scrap.
  • 1922: Tesla lost the lease on Wardenclyffe due to financial problems.
  • 1943: Died in New York City.

    • Key inventions and Contributions

  • AC Induction Motor: A fundamental invention for power generation and distribution.
  • Tesla Coil: A high-voltage, high-frequency transformer used in various applications.
  • Three-Phase AC System: A revolutionary system for transmitting electricity efficiently.
  • Remote Control: Demonstrated with the first remote-controlled boat.
  • Ozone Generator: Patented and used for medical purposes.
  • Wardenclyffe Tower: A pioneering effort in wireless communication.

    • "The mind is sharper and keener in seclusion and uninterrupted solitude", "Be alone, that is the secret of invention; be alone, that is when ideas are born", and "If you want to find the secrets of the universe, think in terms of energy, frequency and vibration".
